Key Vocabulary

Master these essential vocabulary words to better understand and discuss self-talk concepts.

self-talk

(noun) /self tɔːk/ Break it down: self·TALK

The internal dialogue or narration inside your head, sometimes called inner speech

Her positive self-talk helped her overcome her fear of public speaking.

internalize

(verb) /ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz/ Break it down: in·TER·nuh·lize

To make attitudes, behaviors, or beliefs part of your nature

Children gradually internalize their self-talk as they grow older.

ruminate

(verb) /ˈruːmɪneɪt/ Break it down: ROO·muh·nate

To think deeply about something repeatedly, often in a negative way

People who ruminate on their problems often experience more intense feelings of depression.

stigmatize

(verb) /ˈstɪɡmətaɪz/ Break it down: STIG·muh·tize

To treat someone or something as socially unacceptable or disgraceful

Some people stigmatize talking to yourself as a sign of mental instability.

distanced self-talk

(noun phrase) /ˈdɪstənst self tɔːk/ Break it down: DIS·tuhnst·self·TALK

Talking to yourself as if in conversation with another person, using your name or "you"

Distanced self-talk can be especially beneficial for reducing stress during anxiety-inducing tasks.

cognitive behavioral therapy

(noun phrase) /ˈkɒɡnətɪv bɪˈheɪvjərəl ˈθerəpi/ Break it down: COG·nuh·tiv·be·HAV·yuh·rul·THER·uh·pee

A form of psychological treatment focused on changing negative thought patterns and behaviors

Cognitive behavioral therapy helps people identify and change cycles of negative thoughts.

inner speech

(noun phrase) /ˈɪnə spiːtʃ/ Break it down: IN·ner·SPEECH

The silent internal dialogue that occurs in your mind

Most adults experience inner speech rather than speaking out loud to themselves.

motivational

(adjective) /ˌməʊtɪˈveɪʃənl/ Break it down: mo·tuh·VAY·shuh·nuhl

Designed to inspire or encourage action or effort

Engaging in motivational self-talk has been shown to increase focus and boost self-esteem.

compassionate

(adjective) /kəmˈpæʃənət/ Break it down: kuhm·PASH·uh·nuht

Feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others

Cognitive behavioral therapists often teach strategies to replace negative thoughts with more compassionate reflections.

toxic

(adjective) /ˈtɒksɪk/ Break it down: TOK·sik

Extremely harmful or destructive

When negative self-talk becomes too frequent or excessively negative, it can become toxic.

Essential Phrases

Master these key phrases to discuss and practice self-talk effectively.

Discussing Self-Talk

"I catch myself thinking..."

Used to describe becoming aware of your own thoughts

"My inner voice tells me..."

Used to describe what you're saying to yourself internally

Positive Self-Talk

"I can handle this situation..."

Used to build confidence and resilience

"I'm making progress, even if it's small..."

Used to acknowledge growth and maintain motivation

Distanced Self-Talk

"[Your name], you've prepared well for this..."

Used to create psychological distance and reduce stress

"What would you say to a friend in this situation?"

Used to adopt a more compassionate perspective
