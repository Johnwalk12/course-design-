Key Vocabulary

Master these essential vocabulary words to better understand and discuss procrastination.

procrastination

(noun) /prəˌkræstɪˈneɪʃn/ Break it down: pro·CRAS·ti·NAY·shun

The act of delaying or postponing tasks despite expecting negative consequences

Chronic procrastination can lead to increased stress and decreased productivity.

amygdala

(noun) /əˈmɪɡdələ/ Break it down: uh·MIG·duh·luh

A part of the brain involved in emotional processing and threat identification

The amygdala's response to perceived threats can trigger procrastination.

prefrontal cortex

(noun phrase) /ˌpriːˈfrʌntl ˈkɔːteks/ Break it down: PREE·FRUN·tuhl·KOR·teks

The part of the brain responsible for long-term thinking and emotional regulation

When stressed, the prefrontal cortex's ability to regulate emotions can be compromised.

fight or flight

(noun phrase) /faɪt ɔː flaɪt/ Break it down: FIGHT·or·FLIGHT

The body's automatic response to perceived threats or stress

Procrastination can be a response to the fight or flight mechanism triggered by stressful tasks.

self-compassion

(noun) /self kəmˈpæʃn/ Break it down: self·kuhm·PASH·un

Treating oneself with kindness and understanding during difficult times

Practicing self-compassion can help break the cycle of procrastination.

time management

(noun phrase) /taɪm ˈmænɪdʒmənt/ Break it down: TIME·MAN·ij·muhnt

The process of organizing and planning how to divide time between specific activities

Effective time management skills can help prevent procrastination.

stress response

(noun phrase) /stres rɪˈspɒns/ Break it down: STRESS·ri·SPONS

The body's reaction to challenging or threatening situations

Understanding your stress response can help you manage procrastination better.

emotional regulation

(noun phrase) /ɪˈməʊʃənl ˌreɡjuˈleɪʃn/ Break it down: ee·MO·shuh·nuhl·REG·yuh·LAY·shun

The ability to manage and respond to emotional experiences appropriately

Improving emotional regulation can help reduce procrastination tendencies.

task avoidance

(noun phrase) /tɑːsk əˈvɔɪdəns/ Break it down: TASK·uh·VOY·duns

The act of deliberately avoiding or putting off tasks

Task avoidance is a common symptom of procrastination.

self-esteem

(noun) /self ɪˈstiːm/ Break it down: self·ee·STEEM

Confidence in one's own worth or abilities

Low self-esteem can contribute to chronic procrastination.

Essential Phrases

Master these key phrases to discuss and understand procrastination effectively.

Discussing Procrastination

"I've been putting this off because..."

Used to explain the reasons behind procrastination

"The task feels overwhelming because..."

Used to describe the emotional response to challenging tasks

Positive Procrastination

"I can break this down into smaller steps..."

Used to make tasks more manageable and less intimidating

"I'll start with just five minutes..."

Used to overcome initial resistance to starting a task

Distanced Procrastination

"[Your name], you've handled similar tasks before..."

Used to create psychological distance and reduce stress

"What would you advise a friend in this situation?"

Used to gain perspective and find practical solutions
